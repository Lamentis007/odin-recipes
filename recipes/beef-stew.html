Beef Stew

Description

Ingredients

Base Ingredients

  • Beef chuck (stew meat) – ~2 lbs, cut into 1-2" cubes
  • Salt and pepper – to season the beef
  • Flour – ~¼ cup, to coat beef and help thicken
  • Oil – for browning (canola, vegetable, or bacon fat)

Vegetables

  • Yellow onion – 1 large, chopped
  • Garlic – 3–4 cloves, minced
  • Carrots – 3–4 large, peeled and chopped
  • Celery – 2–3 stalks, chopped
  • Potatoes – 3–4 medium (Yukon Gold or Russet), peeled and cubed

Liquid & Flavor

  • Beef broth or stock – ~4 cups (low sodium preferred)
  • Tomato paste – 2 tbsp
  • Bay leaves – 1–2
  • Fresh thyme – 3–4 sprigs (or 1 tsp dried)
  • Worcestershire sauce – 1–2 tbsp
  • Red wine (optional) – ~1 cup (dry red like Cabernet or Merlot)

Optional Upgrades

  • Mushrooms – sautéed separately
  • Parsnips or turnips – chopped
  • Peas – added near the end
  • Paprika or smoked paprika – for added warmth
  • Balsamic vinegar – a splash at the end to brighten flavor
  1. Prep the ingredients
  2. Season and coat beef
  3. Brown the beef
  4. Sauté veggies
  5. Deglaze the pot
  6. Add everything else
  7. Simmer
  8. Add optional ingredients (optional)
  9. Finish and serve

Beef stew is a classic dinner staple in so many households around the world.

There are soup and stew adaptations of beef stew like my favorite Easy Hamburger Soup and cultural variations like Hungarian Goulash, but this classic beef stew recipe is a favorite for me!
