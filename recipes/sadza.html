Sadza (Zimbabwean Maize Meal)

Sadza is a thick maize meal porridge that's the foundation of most Zimbabwean meals.

It's served hot and eaten with meat, vegetables, or sour milk.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups white maize meal (mealie-meal or finely ground cornmeal)
  • 4 cups water (plus more as needed)
  • Pinch of salt (optional)

Steps

  • In a pot, mix 1 cup maize meal with 2 cups cold water to make a smooth slurry.
  • Place the pot on medium heat and stir continuously until it starts to thicken.
  • Once thickened, reduce heat and let it simmer for about 10 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  • Gradually add the remaining 1 cup of maize meal, stirring vigorously to avoid lumps.
  • Adjust thickness by adding more maize meal (to thicken) or hot water (to thin).
  • Cover and simmer on low heat for 5–10 more minutes until fully cooked.
  • Stir one last time and serve hot with stew, vegetables, or sour milk.
